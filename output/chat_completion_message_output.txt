[process of solving]
To solve this problem, we will perform a two-sample t-test to compare the means of the two independent groups. Given the significance level \(\alpha = 0.05\), we will construct a confidence interval for the difference in means (\(\mu_1 - \mu_2\)) and determine whether this interval supports the claim.

We are given:
- IQ scores for medium lead level group: \(72, 89, 92, 85, 89, 97, 83, 92, 95, 111, 91\)
- For high lead level group: \(n_2 = 11, \bar{x}_2 = 89.912, s_2 = 9.528\)

Steps:

1. Compute the sample mean (\(\bar{x}_1\)) and sample standard deviation (\(s_1\)) for the medium lead level group.
2. Use the sample sizes, sample means, and sample standard deviations to compute the standard error and the test statistic.
3. Construct the confidence interval for the difference in means using the formula for the confidence interval for two independent samples.

[equations]
1. Sample mean (\(\bar{x}_1\)): The average IQ score for subjects with medium lead levels.
\[
\bar{x}_1 = \frac{1}{n_1} \sum_{i=1}^{n_1} x_i 
\]
Substitute the given values:
\[
\bar{x}_1 = \frac{72 + 89 + 92 + 85 + 89 + 97 + 83 + 92 + 95 + 111 + 91}{11} = 90.36
\]

2. Sample standard deviation (\(s_1\)): The measure of the amount of variation or dispersion of the IQ scores for medium lead levels.
\[
s_1 = \sqrt{\frac{1}{n_1 - 1} \sum_{i=1}^{n_1} (x_i - \bar{x}_1)^2}
\]
Substitute the given values and \(\bar{x}_1 = 90.36\):
\[
s_1 \approx 10.41
\]

3. Standard Error (SE): The standard error of the difference between two means.
\[
SE = \sqrt{\frac{s_1^2}{n_1} + \frac{s_2^2}{n_2}}
\]
Substitute the given and computed values:
\[
SE = \sqrt{\frac{10.41^2}{11} + \frac{9.528^2}{11}} \approx 4.31
\]

4. Confidence Interval (CI): The range within which we can be confident that the true difference between the population means (\(\mu_1 - \mu_2\)) lies, at a certain confidence level.
\[
CI = (\bar{x}_1 - \bar{x}_2) \pm t_{\alpha/2, \, df} \times SE
\]
Here, the degrees of freedom (df) can be approximated using the formula:
\[
df \approx \frac{\left(\frac{s_1^2}{n_1} + \frac{s_2^2}{n_2}\right)^2}{\frac{(\frac{s_1^2}{n_1})^2}{n_1 - 1} + \frac{(\frac{s_2^2}{n_2})^2}{n_2 - 1}}
\]
Substitute the computed values:
\[
df \approx \frac{\left(\frac{10.41^2}{11} + \frac{9.528^2}{11}\right)^2}{\frac{(\frac{10.41^2}{11})^2}{10} + \frac{(\frac{9.528^2}{11})^2}{10}} \approx 19.94 \approx 20
\]

Using \(t\)-distribution table, find \(t_{\alpha/2, \, df}\) for \(\alpha = 0.05\):
\[
t_{0.025, \, 20} \approx 2.086
\]

5. Constructing the confidence interval (CI):
\[
CI = (90.36 - 89.912) \pm 2.086 \times 4.31
\]
\[
CI = 0.448 \pm 8.99
\]
\[
CI = [-8.542, 8.99]
\]

Therefore, the confidence interval for the difference in means (\(\mu_1 - \mu_2\)) at the 0.05 significance level is approximately \([-8.542, 8.99]\).

This means we are 95% confident that the actual difference in population means falls within this interval.
